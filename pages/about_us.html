Our Story

How Roots Began

Roots began as a small project with 3 households in Makhaza, Khayelitsha in early 2020. Our farmers had extra crops that they would sell to their neighbours but still had excess and needed another way to sell.

We wanted to find a way to support local farmers and create a more equitable food system, so we started by connecting with a few farmers we knew and helping them sell their produce directly to buyers in the city and other areas.

Today, we are continuously expanding to more communities and farmers, whilst we grow our operations and are able to provide more support and tools to better serve our farmers and buyers.

How It Works

Three Steps to Grow

1

Sign Up

Get in touch with us and apply to learn if you can join the network. We also provide resources to help if work still has to be done.

2

Contribute Your Harvest

Farmers contribute their harvest by delivery to our distribution center or through a pickup service we provide at a small cost.

3

Earn Your Share

Farmers receive payment based on quantity and quality of their produce. Small farms are not charged for their first 6 months of selling.

Our Values

What We Stand For

Fairness

We are looking to uplift these communities by ensuring fair compensation, support and transparent practices for all our farmers.

Community First

We prioritize the needs and voices of our community members in all our decisions, ensuring they are at the center of our efforts and that we reinvest in local infrastructure.

Sustainability

We are committed to sustainable farming practices that protect our environment. This includes focusing on soil health, efficient water use, seasonal crops, and encouraging organic practices.

Transparency

Farmers get detailed monthly reports showing exactly what was sold, at what price, where the sales went, and what their share will be.
